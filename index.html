Utility Connection Steps & Costs

To connect your new home to city/county utilities, here are the general steps and associated costs:

  1. Verify Utility Availability: Contact the local city/county utility department to verify water, sewer, and electricity availability.
    Cost: $0 - $50
  2. Submit Application for Utility Connections: Fill out necessary applications for water, sewer, and electrical service.
    Cost: $100 - $500
  3. Sewer Connection Fees: Pay for the tap fee and infrastructure to connect to the city sewer.
    Cost: $3,000 - $6,000
  4. Water Connection Fees: Pay for the water tap fee and any required inspections.
    Cost: $1,000 - $3,500
  5. Electricity Connection: Costs for meter installation and service line.
    Cost: $500 - $2,500
  6. Gas Connection (if applicable): Costs for natural gas line installation.
    Cost: $500 - $2,000
  7. Permit Fees: Building permits for construction and utility connections.
    Cost: $500 - $2,000
  8. Site Preparation Costs: Minor grading and trenching for utilities.
    Cost: $5,000 - $10,000
